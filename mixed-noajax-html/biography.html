Biography

Written by Bob Smith on November 16, 2012.

The french singer Étienne Daho was born the 14th of January in Oran, Algeria . His low and whispery voice and his music made him a pop cult hero. During his career he collaborated, both on stage and in studio, with a lot of artits, including Air, Jane Birkin or Vanessa Paradis

In 1996, he published Eden, a diverse array of electronic-oriented songs that included downtempo ballads, drum 'n' bass-influenced tracks, and HiNRG dance. Re-released subsequently for the anglophone market, Etienne Daho received great reviews with the song "A New World", which was an English language version of "Au Commencement".

In 1998, his greatest hits compilation Best of Singles becomes a double platinumSingles in 1998. With his next album, the number one Corps & armes in 2000, Daho turned in favor of a more orchestral sound with traditional instruments: piano, acoustic guitar, horns. He changed his style another time in favour of a guitar-heavy rock sound with his album Réévolution.
