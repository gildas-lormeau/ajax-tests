Biography

Written by Bob Smith on November 16, 2012.

Denzey was born in Melun, France. He spent most of his childhood in Le MÃ©e-sur-Seine. He formed a band named Prodyge at 15. In June 2000, he reached the final in the broadcast Graines de star and a producer proposed him to release a disc. In 2001, Denzey participated in the Francofolies of La Rochelle, and released his first single "Que te dire" in 2002.

Denzey became famous in May 2003 when he released a second single, "Le mur du son". Then he released his first album, # 1. His single "L'Orphelin" also become a top ten hit in France. He released "Reloaded" in March 2004.

In September 2004, Denzey released his second album simply entitled "Acte II". He released in January 2005 a cover of Joe Dassin entitled "Et si tu n'existais pas" and toured throughout France. He also performed the song used in the movie High School Musical 2 entitled "Double Mise (Bet on it)". His latest single is "L'homme Qu'il Te Faut" released in June 2012.
